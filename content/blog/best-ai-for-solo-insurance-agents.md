## When It's Just You, Everything Falls on You

You're the salesperson, the account manager, the claims assistant, the follow-up machine, the marketer, and the admin — all in one person. You got into insurance because you're good with people and you wanted to build something. But now you spend half your day on tasks that have nothing to do with clients.

Sound familiar? Here's what actually helps.

## The Solo Agent's AI Starter Kit (Free–$50/month)

Before you spend real money, start here:

**ChatGPT or Claude ($0–$25/month):** Use it to draft client emails, summarize policy documents, write marketing content, and brainstorm cross-selling opportunities. This alone can save you 3–5 hours per week.

**Your AMS's built-in features:** Most management systems (Applied Epic, HawkSoft, EZLynx) have automation features that agents never set up. Spend an afternoon configuring renewal reminders, birthday emails, and policy expiration alerts.

**Google Calendar + Calendly ($0–$12/month):** Let clients book their own appointments. Stop playing phone tag.

**Total investment:** $0–$37/month and a Saturday afternoon of setup.

**This works if:** You have fewer than 150 clients and your follow-up workload is manageable.

## The Growing Solo Agent's Stack ($100–$400/month)

When free tools aren't enough:

**Better email automation ($30–$100/month):** ActiveCampaign or Mailchimp for drip campaigns — renewal reminders, holiday greetings, cross-sell sequences. Template-based but consistent.

**AI texting ($50–$150/month):** Tools like Hatch or Podium for automated text follow-ups. Text gets 98% open rates vs. 20% for email. For a solo agent, this is a game-changer.

**AI content creation ($20–$50/month):** Jasper or Copy.ai for creating social media posts, newsletter content, and marketing materials without spending hours writing.

**This works if:** You have 150–300 clients, you're growing, and you need automation but aren't ready for a full system overhaul.

## Solo Agent-Specific Concerns

**"What if it makes a mistake with a client?"**
During the first 30–60 days, you review everything before it goes out. After that, the system has learned your style and your boundaries. It also knows when to escalate to you instead of handling something itself — compliance-sensitive situations, angry clients, complex coverage questions.

**"My clients know me personally. Will they notice?"**
Some will. Most won't care as long as the communication is helpful and accurate. The ones who do notice will appreciate that you respond at 10 PM on a Saturday instead of making them wait until Monday.

**"I don't have IT support."**
SAIOS runs on a dedicated Mac mini in our office. We handle all the technical stuff — updates, maintenance, troubleshooting. You never touch the technology.

**"What about compliance?"**
SAIOS doesn't give insurance advice. It handles communication, scheduling, and prep work. The actual advice, coverage recommendations, and binding decisions always come from you.
