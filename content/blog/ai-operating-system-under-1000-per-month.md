## What $1,000/Month Actually Buys You in AI

If your budget for AI and automation is capped at $1,000/month, you have real options — from cobbling together free tools to deploying a full AI operating system. Here's what each price point gets you.

## Under $100/Month: The Basics

**What you get:**
- ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro ($20–$25/month) for drafting, summarizing, and brainstorming
- Basic email automation through your CRM or Mailchimp ($0–$30/month)
- Calendly for scheduling ($0–$12/month)
- Zapier for simple automations ($0–$20/month)

**What this handles:** Individual task acceleration. You work faster on specific things, but nothing is connected or automated end-to-end.

**Best for:** Businesses just getting started with AI. Annual cost: $600–$1,200.

## $100–$300/Month: Task-Specific AI

**What you get:** Everything above, plus:
- AI-powered texting platform ($50–$150/month) for lead follow-up
- AI writing tools ($20–$50/month) for marketing content
- Enhanced CRM features ($50–$100/month) with lead scoring and pipeline automation

**What this handles:** 2–3 specific workflows are automated. Lead response, follow-up emails, and content creation run faster. But each tool is separate — they don't share context or coordinate.

**Best for:** Growing businesses that know their bottlenecks. Annual cost: $1,200–$3,600.

## $500/Month: Full AI Operating System

**What you get:** SAIOS — a complete AI operating system with five coordinated agents handling:
- All client communication (email, text, chat)
- Lead qualification and follow-up
- Document prep and management
- CRM updates and data management
- Market monitoring and opportunity identification
- After-hours coverage
- Renewal/deadline tracking
- Content creation

**What this handles:** Your entire back office. Not individual tasks — the whole operational layer.

**The catch:** $14,000 upfront setup cost. The $500/month kicks in after a 60-day onboarding period.

**Best for:** Commission-based entrepreneurs with 200+ clients doing $100K+ in revenue. Annual cost: $20,000 Year 1, $6,000/year after.

## $500–$1,000/Month: Operating System + Extras

**What you get:** SAIOS plus:
- Part-time VA for tasks that need a human ($300–$500/month for 5–10 hrs/week)
- Premium marketing tools ($50–$100/month)
- Additional data subscriptions or industry tools

**What this handles:** Everything. AI handles the systematic work, a part-time human handles the exceptions, and you focus entirely on revenue-generating activities.

**Best for:** Agents and entrepreneurs ready to fully step out of operations. Annual cost: $26,000–$32,000 Year 1, $12,000–$18,000/year after.

## Comparing the Real Costs

Here's what most people miss when budgeting for AI: **your time is a cost too.**

| Budget Tier | Monthly Out-of-Pocket | Your Time Managing It | True Monthly Cost* |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Under $100 | $50–$90 | 5–8 hrs/month | $550–$890 |
| $100–$300 | $100–$300 | 3–5 hrs/month | $400–$800 |
| $500 (SAIOS) | $500 | 1–2 hrs/month | $600–$700 |
| $500–$1,000 | $800–$1,000 | 0–1 hrs/month | $800–$1,100 |

*Assumes your time is worth $100/hour

The cheapest option out-of-pocket is often the most expensive in total cost because of the time you spend managing, connecting, and troubleshooting separate tools.

## The Setup Cost Question

"I have $500/month but not $14K upfront."

We get it. Here are your options:

1. **Installment plan:** $19K total, paid in 4 installments ($4,750 each). Higher total but spreads the cash flow.
2. **Start with mid-tier tools** and save toward the setup cost. Use the experience to confirm which workflows matter most to you.
3. **Calculate the ROI first.** If SAIOS helps you close 2 additional deals in the first 3 months (realistic for most agents), the setup cost pays for itself.

## The Bottom Line

Under $1,000/month, you can get anywhere from basic AI assistance to a full operating system that runs your back office. The question isn't which tier you can afford — it's which tier matches the value of your time and the complexity of your operations.

If your time is worth $50+/hour and you're spending 15+ hours/week on admin, the operating system tier is almost certainly the best ROI even though it has the highest out-of-pocket cost.

If your time is worth less or your operations are simpler, start with the basics and grow into more automation as your business grows.
